Extracurricular Honor & Awards

Pan Splitter

Portland, OR | 2023

Problem Statement: Cone type divider unequally distributed between 3 pans created scrap at FKS35 in Part Processing Department

Stakeholders: Part Processing / Heat Treat, Manufacturing Engineer, CAD Design

Financial & Physical Impact: From 2016 - 2022, Increased from 125.6 lb to 1118.5 lb. Costing at least $2800/year

Conveyor Lifter

Portland, OR | 2023

Problem Statement: The Conveyors on Press 4, 7, and M3 need to be repositioned to properly collect parts from the Die Output Chute

Stakeholders: Blanking, Manufacturing Engineering, Maintenance Mechanist, CAD Design Department

Financial & Physical Impact: Each press generated $750 of scrap per year on average. Required labor intensive cleaning and left a disorderly work environment

Assembly Machine Data

Portland, OR | 2023

Problem Statement: In the Cockpit's Chain Assembly raw data, it shows only the number of faults for each machine while we want to see the number of faults/hour

Stakeholders: Continuous Improvement, Assembly Department, Manufacturing Engineers

Financial & Physical Impact: Filtered out over 500,000 rows of data (Number of Faults, Stroke, Runtime, Performance over 3 years)

Cutter Grinder Changeover

Portland, OR | 2023

Problem Statement: The Changeover Process from 4 Cutter Grinders – 18HX and 19HX – (48, 49, 80, 81) took 3 hours per machine on average

Stakeholders: Manufacturing Engineering, Machine Build, Cutter Grinder Department

Financial & Physical Impact: 3 hours down time per machine during changeover, leading to slow productions for both Cutters, affecting Operator’s and Machine Build’s time

CNC Flood Control

Portland, OR | 2023

Problem Statement: Flooding incidents at the CNC Grinders, which were not properly documented and caused significant labor expenses and unground cutters, highlight a failure in the control system and the lack of data on the root cause

Stakeholders: Grinder, Manufacturing Engineer, CAD Design

Financial & Physical Impact: The root cause of the flooding issue was successfully identified and resolved, with approved countermeasures now being procured and implemented to prevent future occurrences
